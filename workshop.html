Workshop
1st Workshop on Neural Combinatorial Optimization
Dec 7th, 2025, San Diego Convention Center, San Diego, USA

Overview

Over the past decade, machine learning has transformed the landscape of combinatorial optimization, giving rise to the emerging Neural Combinatorial Optimization (NCO). Recent advances in NCO have sparked excitement for their potential to revolutionize how we solve complex NP-hard problems by automating algorithm design and providing more efficient, scalable, and adaptable solutions. NCO has demonstrated impressive potential across various subdomains and is on a promising path toward achieving significant real-world impact. The 1st workshop on Neural Combinatorial Optimization (NCO 2025) at ICLR will showcase recent breakthroughs, discuss key challenges, and outline a roadmap to unlock NCO's full practical potential.

The first part of the workshop will bring together experts from both industry and academia to highlight recent advances in NCO and to discuss leading works related to machine learning (e.g., autoregressive models, diffusion models) and operations research (e.g., scheduling, routing, mixed-integer programming). The focus will be on how these approaches can transform NCO applications to create impact across various domains, including transportation, manufacturing, biology, robotics, etc., all involving solving complex CO problems. We will identify key challenges and opportunities for NCO to achieve real-world impact and collaboratively shape the future. The first panel discussion focuses on: "What are the challenges and opportunities for NCO solvers to achieve real-world impact?"

The second part of our workshop will then discuss the emerging trend of developing Foundation Models (FMs) for combinatorial optimization -- a crucial step toward enabling NCO to be easily adapted for complex, multifaceted, real-world scenarios. We will also center on how foundation models from other fields, like Large Language Models (LLMs), can enhance decision-making and uncover new optimizers/knowledge to advance NCO applications. Achieving such breakthroughs would represent a quantum leap in the field for both research and practice.

Scope and Goals

  • Address Real-World Deployment Challenges:

    Identify the key barriers to applying neural combinatorial optimization methods in real-world scenarios. Discussions will focus on overcoming issues such as scalability, generalization, interpretability, and the gap between academic research and industry needs, with the goal of driving real-world impact.

  • Explore Foundation Models in NCO:

    Discuss opportunities and challenges of creating foundation models that can generalize across a wide range of NP-hard combinatorial optimization problems. We will cover key design principles, potential architectures, and how these models can be scaled to real-world applications, drawing parallels to the success of foundation models in other AI domains.

  • Foster Interdisciplinary Collaboration:

    Strengthen connections between NCO, Machine Learning (ML), Operations Research (OR) and various application communities to foster interdisciplinary collaboration, while also enhancing engagement and discussions between NCO researchers and industry participants. The workshop will provide a forum for exchanging ideas, sharing knowledge, and fostering partnerships that can accelerate progress on joint research efforts and applications.

  • Promote Diversity and Inclusion:

    We are dedicated to supporting underrepresented groups in AI, ML, and OR through the tiny paper trac and discussions across groups. Diversity and inclusion have been important guiding principles in the organization of this workshop. Additionally, through poster sessions and other workshop activities, we will actively highlight the contributions of underrepresented researchers, providing them with opportunities to engage with the broader community.

Call For Papers

TL;DR:

  • Length: 4-8 pages (not including references and appendix)
  • Formatting: NeurIPS latex style. Please change the footnote to reflect the name of the workshop.
  • Dual Submission Policy: We accept submissions of ongoing unpublished work and work that is currently under submission.
  • No Proceedings: The workshop is non-archival, which means you can submit the same or extended work as a publication to other venues after the workshop.
  • Submission Deadline: July 25, AoE, 2025
  • Notification Deadline: October 9, 2025
  • Camera-Ready Deadline: November 19, 2025
  • Submission Portal: OpenReview
